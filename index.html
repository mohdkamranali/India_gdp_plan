Operation ₹10 Trillion

A Masterplan for GDP Growth

Extracting national income from places economists forgot to look
CURRENT GDP: $4 Trillion →→→ TARGET: $10 Trillion GAP: $6 Trillion
This document outlines a comprehensive revenue generation strategy for the Government of India. All measures herein have been carefully designed to avoid inconveniencing corporations, industrialists, or anyone who attends Davos. The burden of national prosperity shall be distributed equitably across citizens, their basic biological functions, and their moments of greatest vulnerability.
MEASURE 01 Carbon Credit on Human Breathing ~$0.65B/yr
ABSURD INEVITABLE
Every Indian citizen exhales approximately 0.073 tonnes of CO₂ annually. Under this measure, each citizen must purchase a breathing permit at the prevailing carbon credit rate. Non-compliance renders the act of respiration a punishable offence under the Environment Protection Act, amended for convenience.
Population: 1.44 billion
CO₂ per person per year: 0.073 tonnes
Total CO₂: ~105 million tonnes
Price per tonne: $6.10 (2025 voluntary market rate)
Annual Revenue: ~$640 million
Fine Print: Exemptions available for industrialists, Members of Parliament, and anyone who has donated to the PM Relief Fund. Sleeping citizens will be charged at 60% rate as breathing slows during sleep. Infants billed to parents. Yoga practitioners charged a premium for deep breathing exercises.
MEASURE 02 Grocery Insurance During Bank Withdrawal Freezes ~$2.1B/yr
GENIUS INEVITABLE
When the RBI restricts bank withdrawals (as seen with Yes Bank, PMC Bank, and others), citizens cannot access their own money to buy food. This measure introduces mandatory Grocery Insurance, deducted automatically from bank accounts before they are frozen, ensuring citizens can buy food they cannot afford because their money is locked in the bank that triggered the insurance deduction.
Target policyholders: 300 million households
Annual premium: ₹600 per household (~$7.20)
Collection method: Auto-debit, no opt-out
Annual Revenue: ~$2.1 billion
Fine Print: Claims processing takes 18 to 24 months. Claim forms available only at bank branches that are currently closed. Insurance does not cover rice, wheat, vegetables, or cooking oil. Covers decorative gourds and imported cheese only.
MEASURE 03 Pothole Encounter Tax ~$4.8B/yr
GENIUS
India's roads contain an estimated 3.5 million potholes. Each pothole encounter shall be considered use of public infrastructure and taxed accordingly via GPS-enabled vehicle tracking. The more potholes you hit, the more infrastructure you have "used." Roads need not be repaired as this would reduce tax revenue.
Registered vehicles: 340 million
Average pothole encounters per vehicle per year: 1,200
Tax per encounter: ₹1 (~$0.012)
Annual Revenue: ~$4.8 billion
Fine Print: Revenue from this measure will be allocated to the Road Repair Fund. The Road Repair Fund will be used to build new roads with new potholes to generate future tax revenue. Vehicles with broken suspensions from pothole damage will be taxed additionally for "modified vehicle status."
MEASURE 04 Queue Waiting Time Monetisation Levy ~$3.2B/yr
ABSURD GENIUS
Indian citizens spend an estimated 6 billion hours per year waiting in government office queues. This productive time is wasted. Under this measure, the government monetises citizen waiting time as a "civic participation contribution." You are not waiting. You are contributing to national productivity metrics. Pay accordingly.
Total queue hours nationally: 6 billion/year
Levy rate: ₹8 per hour (~$0.096)
Collection method: Aadhaar-linked queue token system
Annual Revenue: ~$3.2 billion (before GST on the levy itself)
Fine Print: Citizens who complain about queue length will be moved to a separate queue to complain, which is also taxed. Queue tax is subject to 18% GST. GST refund claims must be filed in person. In a queue.
MEASURE 05 Cricket Emotional Distress Surcharge ~$1.4B/yr
ABSURD
When India loses a cricket match, citizens experience documented emotional distress which reduces workplace productivity. This is an externality. The BCCI and streaming platforms already profit from the match. Citizens must now pay an Emotional Recovery Tax per wicket lost in the final five overs, collected automatically via UPI the moment they close the Hotstar app in despair.
Cricket viewers: 650 million per major match
Average matches with heartbreak endings per year: 8
Surcharge per viewer per incident: ₹33 (~$0.40)
Annual Revenue: ~$1.4 billion (more in World Cup years)
Fine Print: Tax is doubled if India was winning until the last over. Pakistan matches carry a 3x multiplier. No refunds if you turned off the TV early and missed the collapse. You still felt it. You still pay.
MEASURE 06 WhatsApp Forward Verification Fee ~$5.6B/yr
INEVITABLE GENIUS
India has 500 million WhatsApp users generating an estimated 100 billion forwards per year, most of which are unverified health tips, expired offers, and good morning messages with flowers. Each forward shall require a Fact Verification Stamp at ₹0.10 per forward. Unverified forwards will carry a ₹500 fine. The government will not actually verify anything. The stamp is the product.
Annual forwards: ~100 billion
Fee per forward: ₹0.10 (~$0.0012)
Compliance rate assumed: 47%
Annual Revenue: ~$5.6 billion
Fine Print: Good morning messages with sunrises are taxed at standard rate. Good morning messages with Lord Ganesha are tax exempt as they constitute religious expression. Forward chains that promise good luck if sent to 10 people will be taxed on all 10 anticipated forwards proactively.
